Oil Defenders

Oil Defenders was my first group project — I was the sole programmer on a team of four, developed over 10 weeks. It's a 3D tower defense game where the player defends oil rigs against waves of tanks and planes.

Development

The goal is to manage resources and strategically place turrets to stop incoming enemies. Resources come from destroying enemies and harvesting oil, but oil rigs themselves can also be destroyed if enemies get through.

There are four buildings — the Oil Rig generates cash over time but can be destroyed, the Laser Turret attacks both ground and air units, the Arc Turret pierces through ground units, and the Tesla Turret chains electricity through multiple enemies. Against them are four enemies — the Tank, the tougher Reinforced Tank, the fast flying Plane, and the stronger Reinforced Plane.

As my first group project, it was a big learning experience in team collaboration and project planning. Communication and scope planning were rough at the start since none of us had worked in a structured team before, but we adapted quickly.

My main role was implementing turret functionality (targeting, firing, chaining effects), setting up enemy pathing and behaviors for ground and air units, integrating resource generation through oil rigs, and debugging and optimizing the gameplay loop.

Explanation

This script manages the enemy wave system, controlling when and where enemies spawn across two lanes for both ground and air units. It:

  • Tracks whether the game is started or paused, controlling when waves advance
  • Manages wave progression, with timers between waves and a per-enemy spawn delay
  • Spawns enemies in the correct lane and type — ground vs. air, left vs. right
  • Keeps track of the current wave index to ensure progression through all waves

This script was the backbone of the game's challenge curve — it taught me how to structure enemy spawns using coroutines, manage timing in gameplay loops, and design systems flexible enough for different wave compositions while staying easy for designers to expand.
