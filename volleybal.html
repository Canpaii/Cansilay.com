Volleyball Game Jam

As one of our end-of-first-year projects, we took part in a summer-themed, 3-day game jam. Our team of four built a local multiplayer volleyball game — I was the sole programmer, responsible for implementing all of the game's systems.

Development

It's a simple local multiplayer game where you try to land the ball on your opponent's side of the field. Despite the short time, we managed to make a fully playable game.

Developing this was genuinely fun — being so restricted on time meant we had to be quick on our feet. Communication was key, since we had to build everything with full intention and avoid wasting each other's time.

Explanation

This script controls the ball's behavior, combining physics-based movement with player input, collision handling, and audio feedback to make it feel dynamic and fun. It:

  • Applies a constant upward force to simulate buoyancy and make the ball feel “floaty”
  • Detects collisions with players and applies custom forces based on their movement, adding extra upward and forward momentum on hits
  • Plays different sound effects for hits and wall collisions, with randomized pitch to keep the audio varied
  • Supports reset functionality, repositioning the ball instantly for each player

This script taught me how much physics tuning and feedback matter for player experience — small adjustments to force multipliers or audio variety made the difference between a ball that felt unresponsive and one that felt lively to hit.
